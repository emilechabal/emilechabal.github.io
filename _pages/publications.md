<a href="francecover.jpg"><img src="/images/francecover.jpg" title="Click here for larger image" alt="Cover image" /></a><span style="font-style: italic;">France </span>(Cambridge: Polity Press, 2020)

This book is an introduction to French history since 1940, organised around six thematic and chronological chapters. Starting from the idea that France is a "paradoxical" nation, it looks at a number of contradictions within recent French history, including the tension between defeat and resistance, state and citizen, and colonialism and anti-colonialism.

I discussed the rationale behind the book at <a href="http://frenchhistorysociety.co.uk/blog/?p=2515">my online book launch event</a> at the Institute of Historical Research. The bilingual website Tocqueville21 also did a review forum on it, with contributions by <a href="https://tocqueville21.com/focus/paradoxes-of-france/">Art Goldhammer</a>, <a href="https://tocqueville21.com/focus/la-tache-de-lhistorien/">Emmanuel Jousse</a>, and a <a href="https://tocqueville21.com/focus/lembarras-du-choix/">response by me</a>.
